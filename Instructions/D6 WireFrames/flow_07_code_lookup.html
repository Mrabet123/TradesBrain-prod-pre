What's the minimum slope for a 3-inch horizontal drain line?

REX · IPC 2021

A 3-inch horizontal drain line requires a minimum slope of ⅛ inch per foot (approximately 1% grade). This ensures adequate flow velocity to carry solids without settling.

For 2½-inch or smaller pipe the minimum slope increases to ¼ inch per foot.

Section 704.1 — Slope of Horizontal Drainage Piping
OFFICIAL CODE TEXT
Horizontal drainage piping shall be installed in uniform alignment at uniform slopes. The slope of a horizontal drainage pipe shall be not less than that indicated in Table 704.1.
