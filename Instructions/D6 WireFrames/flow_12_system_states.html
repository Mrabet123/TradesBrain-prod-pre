!
STOP — POTENTIAL CRACKED HEAT EXCHANGER
This is a carbon monoxide risk. CO is odourless and can be fatal.
1. Shut the furnace down immediately — turn the thermostat to off, then shut off the gas supply at the furnace valve.

Visual indicators of heat exchanger failure in your photo:

Electrician — Stage 3

Neutral bus bar is damaged — looks like a failed terminal block. How do I replace it? [📷 photo of panel]

Diagnosis: Neutral bus bar terminal block failure — visible heat discolouration and cracked insulation at terminal 4. Root cause: high-resistance connection from under-torqued terminal screw, sustained over time.

Solution: Replace the terminal block. Matching block by bus bar manufacturer. If the bus bar itself shows heat damage beyond the terminal — full bus bar replacement required.

NEC 2023 Article 408.55 — Neutral bus bars must be rated for the circuit. [Verify current adoption in your jurisdiction — local amendments may apply]

⚠
SAFETY — LOCKOUT/TAGOUT REQUIRED
Confirm circuit is de-energised and tested dead before touching any component in this panel.
1. Lock out the main breaker with a lockout device.
2. Test all conductors with a calibrated meter — verify dead at the bus bar before working.
3. Do not rely on the breaker position alone — test with meter every time. (NFPA 70E)
